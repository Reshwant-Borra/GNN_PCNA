# Claim and Paper Workflow

## Purpose

Ensure manuscripts, reports, abstracts, captions, and docs do not overstate evidence.

## Agents

- Context.
- Paper Claim.
- Metrics.
- Biological Realism.
- Validation.
- Provenance.
- Contradiction.
- Visual Evidence if figures are referenced.
- Reviewer for hostile review.

## Steps

1. Load claim registry.
2. Load paper draft or target text.
3. Extract scientific claims.
4. Map each claim to a claim ID.
5. Identify unmapped claims.
6. Check allowed wording.
7. Verify current supporting artifacts.
8. Verify metrics and uncertainty.
9. Verify validation classification.
10. Check biological plausibility.
11. Check citations if available.
12. Detect disallowed wording.
13. Produce safe replacements.
14. Request human approval if claim strength changes.

## Disallowed Wording Unless Explicitly Approved

- "validated cryptic pocket"
- "confirmed binding residue"
- "proved"
- "discovered a druggable pocket"
- "MD validates"
- "generalizes broadly"
- "experimentally validated"
- "causal mechanism"

## Pass Criteria

Every claim maps to evidence, wording is allowed, limitations are stated, figures align, and no stale artifacts are cited.
