## Why ff19SB + OPC Is The Scientific Default

1. The ff19SB protein force field was parameterized and validated against the
   OPC water model. Using ff19SB in solvent other than OPC removes the
   self-consistency that was present during force-field training.
2. The OPC water model produces dipole moment, dielectric, density, and
   self-diffusion behavior that better match experiment than TIP3P. For a
   cryptic-pocket study where solvent-mediated dynamics in the AOH1996/IDCL
   region and the candidate Tier 1A windows are central observables, OPC
   reduces a known force-field systematic.
3. Mixing ff19SB with TIP3P has been reported in the literature to over-stabilize
   alpha helices and to over-rigidify side chains, which is exactly the regime
   the Wave 1 candidate-window analysis is sensitive to (RMSF, DCCM, pocket
   accessibility).
4. GAFF2 / AM1-BCC is compatible with OPC at the level of ligand-water
   interactions because the AM1-BCC charges were derived in an implicit-solvent
   context and are commonly used with both TIP3P and OPC in current AMBER
   workflows.

## When ff19SB + TIP3P Is Defensible

A human launch reviewer may select ff19SB + TIP3P as a documented deviation if
all of the following are recorded before launch:

1. Engine-compatibility constraint that prevents OPC use (for example a fixed
   AMBER simulation engine, an HPC environment, or an existing ion parameter
   pipeline that lacks Joung-Cheatham OPC-compatible parameters).
2. Comparison-with-prior-literature constraint that requires the same water
   model as a referenced cryptic-pocket study, with explicit citation.
3. Documented planned-deviation entry that scopes interpretation to "supportive
   computational evidence under the ff19SB + TIP3P setup" and forbids absolute
   thermodynamic, kinetic, or druggability claims.
4. Acknowledgment that ff19SB + TIP3P removes the internal consistency of the
   force-field training and the simulation cannot be used to make claims about
   absolute helix stability, side-chain flexibility, or solvent-mediated
   transitions on this surface.

ff19SB + TIP3P remains a frequently used pairing in the literature for legacy
reasons. It is not categorically disqualified, but for this study it is a
secondary choice rather than the scientific default.

## Why Not ff14SB

ff14SB + TIP3P is the long-standing Amber default in many older PCNA papers
and would also be defensible. It is not the recommended default here because:

1. ff19SB is the current Simmerling-lab-recommended protein force field for new
   work.
2. Phase 5 Wave 1 has not yet committed any equilibrated topologies, so there
   is no operational cost to choosing the more recent force field.
3. ff14SB has known systematic helix-bias issues for some residue classes that
   are exactly the residue classes adjacent to the IDCL and several Tier 1A
   windows.

A human launch reviewer may still select ff14SB + TIP3P as a documented
deviation if reproducing prior PCNA MD literature is a primary goal. That
choice must be recorded and labeled as a deviation.

## Required Items Before Any Force-Field Decision Is Used For MD

A future human launch authorization record must include:

1. Named force-field/water pairing: `ff19SB + OPC` (default), `ff19SB + TIP3P`
   (allowed deviation), or `ff14SB + TIP3P` (allowed deviation).
2. Ion parameter set used for the chosen water model.
3. Explicit ZQZ net-charge decision from `reports/phase5/zqz_chemistry_decision_20260611.md`.
4. Box size, salt concentration, neutralization scheme, temperature, pressure,
   thermostat/barostat, hydrogen mass repartitioning policy (if any), and
   integrator timestep.
5. Reference to the same policy across all three systems
   (`8gla_holo_zqz`, `8gla_apo_from_holo`, `1axc_apo_from_p21`) so MD comparisons
   are valid under doc 13 hard rules.
6. Short benchmark/preflight plan that captures atom counts, ns/day, and
   trajectory stability before authorizing full production.
